X Risk Reduction

Clean up your X history for safety and reputation — fast.

No automation. No account access. You stay in control.

Time required: 30–120 minutes

This does NOT do: erase the internet or guarantee anonymity

Warning: deleting posts is irreversible.

Optional: Download your X archive

Downloading your archive gives you a personal backup before deleting anything. This is optional, but recommended if you’re anxious about losing history.

  1. Go to Settings and privacy
  2. Select Your account
  3. Tap Download an archive of your data
  4. Confirm your password and request the archive
  5. When it’s ready, X will provide a download link

Step 0 — Set your priority

Answer these three questions (mentally or on paper). Don’t overthink.

  • Priority: Safety first / Reputation first / Balanced
  • Account visibility: Public / Protected
  • Purge style: Surgical (only risky) / Aggressive (anything that fails screenshots)

Skeptical? “Why isn’t this automatic?”

Short answer: automatic scanning is less accurate, less safe, and more likely to backfire.

  • This tool can’t see your posts and doesn’t ask for account access.
  • AI can misread sarcasm, context, or personal history.
  • Bulk automation on X is rate-limited and can create account risk.
  • Most real harm comes from a small number of posts.

Tier 2 can add OAuth automation later. Tier 1 is about fast, controlled risk reduction.

Step 2 — The Top-25 Risk Purge

This step is guided — not automated. This tool does not scan your account. You identify the posts; the method keeps you focused.

How to find your Top-25

  • Start with what’s already visible: pinned posts, high-reply posts, quote-tweet magnets.
  • Run focused searches: use Step 3 search patterns to surface conflict, personal info, emotional spikes.
  • Apply the risk test: if any rule hits, it’s a candidate.
  • Stop at 25. More is not better.

Risk test (any one triggers = high risk):

  • Reveals location, routine, health, family, or money
  • Reads badly without context
  • Shows anger, insults, or threats
  • Would hurt you if screenshot by someone hostile

Step 3 — Fast Keyword Sweeps

Only do this if you still have time.

How to search your own posts

  1. Open X and go to your profile.
  2. Use the search bar and type:

from:yourusername keyword

Example: from:yourusername address

This controls future context. It’s not “branding.”

Bio rule

  • Remove employer specifics, schedules, locations, and routine clues.
  • Keep it boring. Boring is safe.

Maintenance rule: If a post wouldn’t be okay as a screenshot on someone else’s account, don’t post it.
